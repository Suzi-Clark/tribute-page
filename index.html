Born: July 24th, 1897
Disappeared: July 2nd, 1937
Declared dead: January 5th, 1939

Amelia Earhart

The first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.

Amelia Earhart wrote a range of magazine articles, newspaper columns and essays during her lifetime, as well as publishing two books: 20 Hrs. 40 Min.: Our Flight in the Friendship (1928) and The Fun of It (1932). A third and final book called Last Flight (1937) was published after her disappearance earlier that year. Last Flight was compiled by her husband from journal entries Earhart had been sending back to the USA during her world flight attempt.
These days, Amelia Earhart is fondly remembered not just for her achievements in the field of aviation, but also for her contribution to early 20th century feminism including her instrumental role in the foundation of the Nintey-Nines, an organisation for female pilots that still exists today.

Records set by Amelia Earhart

  • 1922 - Woman's world altitude record (14,000 ft)
  • 1928 - First woman to fly the Atlantic Ocean
  • 1932 - First woman to cross the United States in an autogyro
  • 1932 - First woman to fly the Atlantic solo
  • 1932 - First woman to receive the Distinguished Flying Cross
  • 1932 - First woman to fly nonstop, coast-to-coast across the U.S.
  • 1933 - Women's speed transcontinental record (1933)
  • 1935 - First person to fly solo between Honolulu, Hawaii, and Oakland, California and first person to fly solo nonstop from Mexico City to Newark, New Jersey
  • 1937 - Speed record for east-to-west flight from Oakland, California, to Honolulu, Hawai
  • 1937 - First person to fly solo from the Red Sea to Karachi
